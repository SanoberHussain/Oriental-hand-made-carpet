Quality

Oriental carpets are mainly made from sheep’s wool, whose quality varies by breed, climate, altitude, and shearing practices. Wool quality depends on its location on the sheep and whether it’s from spring or autumn shearing. Dyes also influence quality, as their source, composition, and application determine the richness and fastness of colors.

Design

Oriental carpet designs are created by varying the colors of hand-tied wool knots, resulting in countless motifs and patterns. Broadly, they are classified as rectilinear (bold, geometric forms, coarsely woven by villages/nomads) or curvilinear (intricate, fluid floral patterns requiring fine weave). Despite the variety, all share striking similarities across history and origin.

Patterns

Carpet designs are organized either as a single central motif or as repeating patterns across the field. The most common is the medallion design, with a central figure and matching corners. Repeating patterns may include lozenge shapes with floral figures and “fish” motifs. Some, like Chakhansur rugs, stand out with bright colors and Turkmen-inspired designs.

Regions

Carpet designs may feature a single central motif or repeating patterns. The most common is the medallion design, with a central figure and corner elements. Others use lozenge shapes with floral and “fish” motifs repeated throughout. Some, like Chakhansur rugs, are unique for their bright colors and Turkmen-inspired patterns.
